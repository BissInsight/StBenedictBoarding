FAQ

What are the age criteria for admission to St. Benedict Boys' Hostel?

Admission to St. Benedict Boys' Hostel is generally open to boys who are enrolled as students in St. Antony's Public School and meet the minimum age requirement as specified by the school's hostel guidelines. Please refer to the school's admission brochure or contact the hostel warden for the specific age criteria for the current academic year.

What is the procedure for applying for admission to the hostel?

The application process usually involves obtaining a hostel application form from the school office or website. You will need to fill out the form completely, attach the necessary documents (such as the student's school ID, photographs, and medical certificate), and submit it to the school administration along with the prescribed fee. Please check the school's admission guidelines for the detailed procedure and required documents.

What are the rules and regulations that students in the hostel must follow?

The hostel has a set of rules and regulations designed to ensure discipline, safety, and a conducive living environment for all residents. These rules typically cover aspects like study hours, meal timings, curfew, dress code within the hostel, use of electronic devices, and behavior. Students and parents are expected to read and adhere to these rules.

What kind of meals are provided in the hostel mess?

The hostel mess usually provides nutritious and balanced meals, including breakfast, lunch, and dinner. The menu is generally planned to cater to the dietary needs of growing boys and may include a variety of vegetarian and non-vegetarian options. The mess schedule and menu are usually displayed in the dining hall.

How is the safety and security of the students ensured in the hostel?

The safety and security of the students are a top priority. The hostel typically has security personnel on duty, CCTV surveillance in common areas, and a strict entry and exit policy. The resident warden is also responsible for the well-being and safety of the students.

What kind of academic support is available for the students in the hostel?

The hostel usually provides a conducive environment for studies. There are designated study hours, and sometimes provisions for supervised study sessions or tuitions may be available. The warden and teachers often provide guidance and support to the students in their academic pursuits.

Can parents visit their children in the hostel? What are the visiting hours?

Parents are usually allowed to visit their children on designated days and during specific visiting hours. These timings are put in place to minimize disruption to the students' study schedules and hostel routine. Parents are generally required to register their visit with the warden upon arrival. Please check the hostel rules for the current visiting schedule.

What is the fee structure for the hostel? What are the modes of payment?

The hostel fee structure varies and usually includes charges for accommodation, food, and other amenities. The fee schedule and payment modes (such as cash, cheque, or online transfer) are typically outlined in the school's fee circular or can be obtained from the school office. It's important to understand the payment deadlines and any applicable late fee charges.
